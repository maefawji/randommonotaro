Drift Hardware Store

MONOTARO is a massive Japanese online hardware and industrial supply store. In this website, I collected the entire category structure of the site in advance, and display products by randomly selecting from its lowest-level categories.

Sometimes I want ideas grounded in ready-made products. In those moments, instead of going to a physical store, I open MONOTARO and browse for components, tools, or materials that might be useful. However, I noticed that I always ended up returning to the same categories out of habit. This project was created as a tool to break away from those patterns and dig more deeply through the catalog.

The layout itself also incorporates randomness and contrast, allowing the products to appear less as information and more as objects. Images with complex backgrounds can be excluded in order to emphasize this object-like quality.

Since loading takes some time, previously fetched images are allowed to reappear. Unexpectedly, this created a kind of musical repetition. Rather than being completely random, previously seen objects gradually return and circulate again.

In the end, the website became something closer to a generative environment, where MONOTARO’s products continuously drift, repeat, and recompose themselves — somewhat reminiscent of Bloom by Brian Eno.
